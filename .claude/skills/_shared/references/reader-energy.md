# Reader energy — the goal-5 surface doctrine

How the essay earns the reader's lean-in, momentum, and leaving-sentence (north-star goal 5 —
see `scoring-rubric.md`). Instructions to all three phases: **Design** picks the energy
register and writes the title-lead candidates (Step 8b), **Compose** executes the chosen pair
(hook-first lead + signature lines), **Edit** defends it (6H defensive-open guard, pass-7 hook
check, surface jurisdiction). The energy contract binds the **SURFACE** — title, cover
caption, section headers, the lead's first paragraph, declared signature lines — and never
relaxes goals 1-4 on the body. Conflicts resolve **accuracy-first for factual defects,
energy-first for style/count on the surface**.

## 1. The five energy registers

Every essay declares ONE register (Phase 1, `title-lead-candidates.md`). All five ride the
same locked spine facts — a register changes the order and polarity of delivery, never the
evidence.

- **discovery** — the lead hands the reader a fact they did not know and could not have
  guessed. Fits when the patent record holds an unknown fact (a date, a signature, a
  mechanism) that reorders a public story. Default register when in doubt.
- **tension** — two facts the reader can check collide in ¶1. Fits when patent evidence and a
  corporate narrative pull in opposite directions (the natural mate of the
  `corporate-narrative-friction` hook).
- **contrarian** — the consensus read is stated in one sentence, then inverted by the
  evidence. Fits when the crowd already has a take and the filing contradicts it; requires a
  real, citable consensus, never a strawman.
- **insider** — the reader gains a privileged view: reading the primary document almost
  nobody else opened, over the essayist's shoulder. Fits when access itself is the value.
- **stakes** — the cost or payoff leads: one number (money, latency, capacity) carries ¶1 on
  its own. Fits when that figure IS the thesis.

## 2. The discovery-first lead pattern (binds Compose)

1. **¶1 delivers the chosen register's beat** — the astonishing thing, the collision, the
   inversion, the privileged view, or the number. Nothing queues in front of it.
2. **Verdict-insurance facts may not precede the beat.** Status labels ("pending
   application", "not yet granted"), liens and collateral, examiner rejections, "still not an
   asset" framings are real facts and they STAY — but they price the discovery after it
   lands, never before.
3. **The full evidence-proportionate call lands by the END of the lead section** — two-sided
   per 6G, insurance included. Hook-first is a reordering inside the lead, not a license to
   defer the call past it (pass-7 item 1 checks both halves).

## 3. Defensive-open (the named failure)

**Definition:** insurance-before-discovery — one or more verdict-insurance facts stacked
ahead of the lead's discovery beat. The report-genre tell: the essay protects itself before
it gives the reader a reason to stay. Flagged by pass-6 **6H** (judgment) and `SURF-004`
(mechanical warn).

**Before** — etched-us20240378175 v7 §1, three defensive facts stacked ahead of the payoff:

> The memory half of Etched's architecture story has been in writing since 10 May 2023, in
> the company's first patent filing, signed by both co-founders. Three years on, that
> document is still not an asset. It is a pending application, still being argued with a
> patent examiner, and pledged alongside the rest of the company's patent stack as loan
> collateral. Meanwhile the philosophy it wrote down is presented on stage as shipping
> hardware.

The opening fact lands flat as a record note, then three insurance facts (not an asset; still
argued with an examiner; pledged as collateral) queue up before the payoff sentence. A
scroller is gone by "pending application".

**After** — the same facts, reversed polarity: astonishing thing first, priced second:

> Two years before Etched pitched its no-layer memory philosophy on stage, both co-founders
> had signed that idea into the company's first patent filing, dated 10 May 2023. The stage
> version arrived in July 2026 as shipping hardware. The written version is three years
> older, and it is still not an asset: a pending application, argued with an examiner to this
> day, and pledged as loan collateral in the meantime.

Nothing was cut and nothing was softened; the discovery leads, the insurance prices it.

## 4. Feed-context rules (the X card is the first reader)

- **Title ≤ 70 characters** (spaces included). The feed card truncates; the 9-14 word
  convention in `essay-en-composer/references/x-articles-format-en.md` still shapes the
  title, but when words and characters conflict, characters win. *(gate: `SURF-001` warn.)*
- **Cover caption numeral budget: ≤ 6 distinct reference numerals.** A cover caption dense
  with part numbers reads as a parts list in the feed; move the inventory to a body caption.
  *(gate: `SURF-003` warn.)*
- **First-two-lines test.** The card shows the title plus roughly the first two lines of ¶1.
  Those lines alone must earn the tap: no throat-clearing, no insurance, no "this essay
  examines". If the register's beat has not started by line two, it is too late.

## 5. Protected signature lines

- The composer declares **up to 3 signature lines** in `thesis-trace.md` under
  `## Signature lines` (exact strings) — the aphoristic, repeatable sentences the reader
  carries out, usually the `reader_sentence` or its close siblings.
- **Echo/count rules exempt declared lines:** pass-2 claim-repetition, pass-7
  thesis-restatement, and repetition-style judgments neither count their recurrences nor
  recommend rewording them.
- **Factual review still applies in full:** a signature line with a grounding, scope, or
  causality defect is a normal pass-3/4 finding. Protection covers style and count, never
  accuracy.

## 6. Attention budget (procedure is pricing, not plot)

The named failure this section defends against (first seen: etched-us20240378175-r2 v5,
class `procedure-overweight-lead`): the lead opened on the discovery beat — so §3
defensive-open passed — and then process narration (examiner fights, fees, RCE mechanics,
spend-to-keep-alive riffs, liens) piled up in ¶2–3 and again in a mid-essay section and the
closing. Every sentence was individually grounded, every ORDER check passed, and the reader
still left before the technology arrived. Defensive-open constrains order; this section
constrains VOLUME and PLACEMENT.

- **Reader-payload-first lead.** The lead answers *what the invention does and what that
  changes* before *what it costs and where it stands*. Status/insurance content inside the
  lead is capped at the two-sided call itself — status LABELS ("still pending", "the office
  hasn't said yes") are call material and always allowed; process NARRATION (fee amounts,
  RCE mechanics, who-paid-when, lien walks) is not lead material at all.
- **One structural home for procedure.** Prosecution/finance material lives in exactly one
  pricing section, plus at most one lead clause and the closing's verdict recap. It never
  distributes across lead + body + closing — when the same money/process beat appears in
  three places, the essay's plot has become the paperwork.
- **Motif budget, not sentence budget.** When the edition brief budgets a topic ("exactly
  ONE label sentence"), paraphrase echoes of the same motif (keeps-paying / expensive-to-
  keep-alive / fee-paid-to-argue variants) draw on the SAME budget. Declared signature
  lines (§5) are exempt from the count, as everywhere.
- **Verdict frame ≠ narrative frame.** An edition contract may define the VERDICT by the
  document's treatment (collateral, continued prosecution) — that never licenses treatment
  as the STORY structure. The spine's subject stays the invention; treatment is predicate.
- **The concession beat spends attention too (steelman-overweight).** The steelman is a
  concession, and elaborating a concession **primes the objection** ("don't think of an
  elephant" — the negation/framing effect), so a concede beat that out-spends its own rebuttal
  leaves the reader carrying the counter, not the invention. The steelman is therefore
  **two-sided**, symmetric with 6G over-hedge ↔ overreach: `steelman-absent` (missing/truism)
  AND `steelman-overweight` (present and specific but over-elaborated — the concession
  out-weighs the affirmative core, re-spends a caveat an earlier section already made, or
  narrates spend/procedure inside the beat). The budget is a **ratio, not a length**: the
  affirmative core carries ≥ the concession; the beat is net-new; the spend/procedure lexicon
  stays out of it. Distinct from `procedure-overweight-lead`, which binds the lead and the
  essay-wide motif — a steelman can be overweight with zero spend motif and outside the lead
  (a long claim-scope dissection that never returns to the core), which SURF-005/006 do not
  reach. First seen: intel-us20250266395 v5 §5 + etched-us20240378175-r2 v5 §5, both
  human-caught post-accept (proposal `2026-07-06-steelman-two-sided`).
- **Control-group heuristic.** When the fixed-schema artifacts (owner briefing, promo pack)
  read better than the essay, suspect attention allocation, not facts — long-form is the
  only artifact where allocation is unconstrained.

Instruments: `gate_surface` SURF-005 (procedure-narration sentences in the lead section),
SURF-006 (essay-wide spend-motif count), and **SURF-007** (spend/procedure motif inside the
concession/steelman section — the `steelman-overweight` mechanical half), all warn; pass-6
**6I attention-budget guard** and pass-7 check 3 (`steelman-overweight`) are the judgment
backstops; Phase 1 declares per-section `payload` tags in the spine → section trace
(thesis-spine template).

## 7. Jurisdiction (recap)

The **accuracy contract** (unchanged) binds the body: anchors, quotes, claim scope,
attribution, evidence levels, hedge symmetry; grounding fix priority anchor → narrow → label
→ cut. The **energy contract** binds the surface, owned by the leaving-sentence
(`reader_sentence` in `input/essay-context.md`, see `reader-profile.md`) plus the declared
register. Review flags energy violations (defensive-open, gap-framed headers that bury the
discovery, attention-budget breaches) and factual errors on the surface — it does not sand
surface style or count protected lines.
