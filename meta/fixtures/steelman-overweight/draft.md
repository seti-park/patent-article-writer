# A Filing That Locks One Step Before the Rest

## The Filing Locks a Step No One Else Claims

The patent claims a specific ordering [0012]: the controller writes to the buffer
before the array reads it, and no other filing in the docket claims that exact
sequence. The inventor named the step directly in the independent claim, and the
description repeats it twice more [0018]. That ordering is the whole thesis in one
sentence, and it is written down years before any product shipped.

## The Objection, Read Cold

This section concedes the strongest objection directly: no single claim here has
issued yet, and the filing could still narrow under examination. Read cold, the
filing's counterparts in Japan, Korea, China and Germany are fee money a company
does not usually spend on ideas it considers dead. That spending pattern is the
whole paragraph's argument, repeated once more for emphasis before the essay moves
on to anything else.

## The Core Holds Anyway

The ordering claim is what survives regardless of how the objection above lands.
The controller-first sequence is written down, dated, and distinct from every cited
reference the examiner has raised so far. Nothing in the objection above touches
that sequence directly, which is why the core claim keeps its footing either way.
