# Title-Lead Candidates

**reader_sentence** (from `input/essay-context.md`; drafted here if absent):
> Tesla had the 70-millisecond decision on file at the patent office months before it announced it.

## discovery

- **title**: Tesla Filed the 70ms Decision Months Before Announcing It
- **lead**: The architecture behind Tesla's 70-millisecond airbag claim was sitting at the patent office months before the stage announcement. The filing describes a vision path that decides deployment before the impact begins, not after. The number the presentation celebrated had already been written down.
- **rationale**: Delivers the reader_sentence's unknown fact (the filing predates the announcement) as ¶1's payoff.

## tension

- **title**: Tesla Claims 70ms. The Industry Baseline Is 10. Both Are Real
- **lead**: Tesla says its system decides 70 milliseconds earlier; the accelerometer baseline the industry ships responds in about 10. Both numbers are real, and they cannot describe the same architecture. The patent is where the two stop contradicting each other.
- **rationale**: Collides the announcement number with the baseline the reader can check; the reader_sentence resolves the collision.

## contrarian

- **title**: The 70ms Number Is Not Marketing. The Patent Does the Math
- **lead**: The consensus read of Tesla's 70-millisecond claim was marketing rounding. The patent filed months earlier does the arithmetic in claim language: a vision path deciding deployment before impact. The skeptics were arguing with a document they had not read.
- **rationale**: States the consensus in one sentence, then inverts it with the filing — the reader leaves able to correct the crowd.

## insider

- **title**: Reading the Patent Behind Tesla's 70ms Airbag Claim
- **lead**: Almost nobody who repeated Tesla's 70-millisecond claim has read the document it comes from. US 2026/0125022 A1 is that document, and it says more than the presentation did. Here is what it commits to, clause by clause.
- **rationale**: Privileged-view framing; the reader_sentence becomes something the reader saw for themselves.

## stakes

- **title**: Seventy Milliseconds Is the Margin an Airbag Lives On
- **lead**: An airbag that decides 70 milliseconds earlier is deciding before the crash, not during it. That margin is the difference the whole filing exists to claim. Tesla put it in writing months before putting it on stage.
- **rationale**: Leads with the payoff number itself; the reader_sentence prices what the number buys.

---

recommended: discovery
why: The filing-before-announcement fact is verifiable, unknown to the target reader, and carries the reader_sentence in one beat; tension is the fallback if the edition foregrounds the baseline fight.
