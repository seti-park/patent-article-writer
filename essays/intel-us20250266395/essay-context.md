# Essay context — US20250266395A1 (Intel multi-die bridge assemblies)

## Edition framing

- **News hook**: Intel's EMIB-T packaging is in the news (HBM4/UCIe support, fab rollout
  reported for 2026; TSV bridge dies delivering power from the package bottom). The reader
  arrives asking: "is this patent the EMIB-T story, or something more?"
- **Working thesis direction** (Phase 1 may sharpen, not soften): while the market watches
  EMIB-T productize, this application stakes out the NEXT assembly flow — bond the bridge
  to the dies FIRST (hybrid bonding at 1-10 µm pitch), TEST the multi-die assembly before
  any substrate is committed, THEN drop the bridge into a substrate cavity and power it
  through TSVs/TGVs from the cavity floor.
- The economically interesting move is the **pre-substrate known-good test** (FIG. 11,
  [0061]-[0062]): in 12-die, reticle-busting AI packages, one bad die scrapping a finished
  package is the yield nightmare this flow attacks. Give this at least equal weight to the
  hybrid-bonding pitch numbers.
- Secondary tie-in: glass-core substrate + TGV (claim 17), glass bridge, glass frame —
  connects to Intel's publicized glass-substrate roadmap.

## Accuracy guards (do not overreach)

- The patent NEVER uses the term "EMIB" or "EMIB-T". Do not assert "this is EMIB-T" or
  "this is EMIB-T's successor" as fact. Correct posture: same problem family and same
  TSV-bridge power-delivery idea as EMIB-T ([0024], [0035]), but with the assembly order
  inverted relative to Intel's publicly described chips-last EMIB flow.
- This is a published application (filed 2024-02-20, published 2025-08-21), not a granted
  patent and not a product roadmap. Intel files defensively across its option space; one
  filing is not evidence of productization. Inventor roster (Mahajan et al., Intel
  packaging leadership) supports "mainline packaging org", nothing stronger.
- EMIB-T facts (voltage droop / cantilevered power path, HBM4/UCIe targets, rollout
  timing) are EXTERNAL context — they must go through fact-check-log with sources, not be
  attributed to the patent text.

## Audience note

- Default reader-profile applies (curious retail investor). The reader has likely seen
  EMIB-T headlines but cannot define a bridge die, TSV, or hybrid bonding — each needs a
  one-clause gloss on first use.
