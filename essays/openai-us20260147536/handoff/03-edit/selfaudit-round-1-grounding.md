## Notes on the one flagged row (#37)

- **Location**: §4 body, "A VMM is the document's shorthand for vector-matrix multiplication,
  the operation that dominates AI training and inference [0002]."
- **Verdict**: OVERREACHED-BEYOND-ANCHOR. [0002] is a generic linear-algebra framing
  ("Matrix-vector multiplication is a fundamental computational operation in many areas of
  science and engineering, including linear algebra, signal processing, and machine
  learning") — it does not itself assert that the operation "dominates AI training and
  inference." That specific claim is fully supported one paragraph later, at [0004]: "AI...
  techniques frequently rely on multilayer neural networks... inference and training often
  involve repeated application of matrix-vector and matrix-matrix multiplications... These
  workloads commonly process large batches of data in parallel."
- **Recommended fix**: re-anchor the clause to [0004] (a strictly better-fitting span exists
  in the same document) — no change to the prose's substance is needed, only the citation.
