# Thesis Spine

## Selected thesis

**One-line spine**:
> OpenAI's first-published chip patent claims a compute-in-memory engine that does the expensive half of floating-point matrix math on plain integer adders, and its transistor-level depth, dated a year before the Broadcom announcement, is primary evidence that "OpenAI-designed" silicon is literal engineering.

Subject check (verdict frame ≠ narrative frame): the spine's subject is the invention (the align-once, integer-accumulate CIM macro); the filing's status and timeline are predicate/pricing, not plot.

## Adversarial defense

**Strongest objection**: The pending claims do not cover the story the description sells. Independent claims 1 and 20 claim only the align-by-shifting + integer-adder-tree structure; the format-flexible mode decoding unit, the FP4/FP6/FP8 agility, and the dual-bank double-buffering live in the description and aspects (and the adjacent claim block 11-19 is canceled in this publication). Moreover, block-scaled accumulation over shared-exponent blocks is established practice — the OCP MX standard (2023) is built on exactly that idea — so an informed reader can dismiss the claimed core as an implementation of a known technique.

**Mitigation**: §2 builds the mechanism walk strictly on the claim-1 triad, quoting the `[0011]` spans that mirror the claim wording. §3 carries the explicit claimed-vs-described beat: it names what these claims reach (shift-calc unit, register sizing = max exponent + mantissa bits, logarithmic mux tree; claims 2-10, 20-28) and what they do not (mode decoding, dual banks — description/aspect material), using the Claim scope map's sought-* vocabulary, and it separates the MX standard (a data FORMAT everyone shares) from what is claimed here (specific CIRCUIT structures that execute such blocks natively inside a memory macro). Then it returns to the affirmative core: whichever claims survive examination, the document itself is a dated, assigned, tape-out-depth record of silicon capability inside OpenAI, and that is what the thesis rests on.

**Residual risk**: Acknowledged — the claims of a pending application can be amended or rejected in examination, and the essay does not know where the inventors sat on the provisional date (Rain AI lineage is press-reported). §5 states both bounds once, on registry facts and labeled evidence levels.

**Steelman beat**: §3, concede-and-return. The concession is specific and bounded: exactly which headline features are outside this claim set, plus the MX-is-prior-format point — and it is net-new (the pending-status caveat is spent in §5, not here; no re-spend). The return: the claimed circuit specifics plus the primary-source depth carry at least as much attention as the concession. No spend/procedure lexicon inside the beat. Generic "patents don't guarantee products" truism is banned and unused.
