## Candidate 1: The paper trail of OpenAI's silicon turn

**Statement**: OpenAI's first-published chip patent claims a compute-in-memory engine that does the expensive half of floating-point matrix math on plain integer adders, and its transistor-level depth, dated a year before the Broadcom announcement, is primary evidence that "OpenAI-designed" silicon is literal engineering, not press-release framing.

**Framing**: primary-document reveal. Let the filing's priority date and circuit depth reorder the public "OpenAI is a chip buyer" narrative, with the claimed mechanism (align once, add as integers) as the payload the reader actually learns.

**Evidence required**:
- Claimed mechanism (claim 1/20 triad: FP primitive product, mantissa alignment by shifting, integer adder tree)
- Registry timeline (provisional 2024-11-22 vs Broadcom announcement 2025-10-13, Jalapeño 2026-06-24)
- Silicon-depth evidence (latch-level bitcells, timing, scan chains)
- External baseline (conventional conversion pipelines / GPU practice; OCP MX format standard)

**Evidence available in invention-summary**:
- ✓ Claims anchor (q-0011-1/2/3 mirror claim 1's wording)
- ✓ Problem spans (q-0005-1/2/3, q-0133-2)
- ✓ Effect spans (q-0131-1, q-0145-1, q-0147-1, q-0154-1/2/3, q-0183-1)
- ✓ Silicon-depth spans (q-0118-1) + reference-number density
- ✓ External baseline via fact-check-log (openai-broadcom-10gw, ocp-mx-spec-2023-09)

**Structural tension**: the public story says OpenAI started designing chips with Broadcom (announced October 2025); the patent record says circuit-level design work carried an OpenAI-assigned priority date of November 2024, and it is deep enough to include test scan chains and clock-margin scripts.

**Risks**:
- Where the inventors sat at the provisional date is NOT established (Rain AI lineage is press-reported only); the essay must not claim OpenAI employed them in Nov 2024, only that the priority date and assignment are OpenAI's.
- Claim-scope drift: the format-flexibility story is description material, not the pending claims (mitigated by the Claim scope map trap).

**Grounding (4-axis — draft, locked in Step 4)**:
- Claims anchor: 청구항 1 — the (b)/(c) limitations, verbatim at `[0011]`: "a functional block configured to align mantissa bits of primitive products generated by column cells in the computing unit by shifting the mantissa bits" + "an adder tree configured to output an accumulation value of the primitive products in an integer format by adding the shifted mantissa bits"
- Problem anchor: `[0005]` "Each conversion step requires additional scaling, rounding, and saturation logic, which adds area, power, and latency to already dense compute fabrics..."
- Effect anchor: `[0006]` "without requiring separate conversion pipelines or dedicated cores for each format" + `[0147]` one VMM product every cycle
- Baseline-difference anchor: OpenAI's current compute substrate (GPU tensor-core accelerators; data moved from memory to arithmetic cores, characterized at `[0133]`) and the Broadcom "OpenAI-designed accelerators" narrative (2025-10-13) vs an in-memory, integer-domain-accumulating macro on file since 2024-11-22 (industry-narrative + architecture comparison)

**Q7 hook pattern (draft, hard-gated in Step 5)**:
- corporate-narrative-friction
- anchor: OpenAI/Broadcom announcement of "OpenAI-designed AI accelerators" (2025-10-13) + Jalapeño reveal (2026-06-24) vs provisional priority date 2024-11-22 — the transistor-level design record predates the public design narrative by ~11 months. Payload condition satisfied: ¶1 can state what the invention does (does math inside the memory array; floating-point products summed by integer adders).

**Adversarial defense (draft, locked in Step 6)**:
- Strongest objection: the claims of this publication cover only the align-and-integer-accumulate structure, not the format-flexible chip the description narrates, and block-scaled accumulation is an established idea (OCP MX standardized shared-scale blocks in 2023).
- Mitigation: build the mechanism section strictly on claim-1 wording; run a dedicated claimed-vs-described beat with the map's sought-* vocabulary; distinguish format standard (data layout) from claimed circuits (shift-calc unit, register sizing, log mux tree).

## Candidate 2: Floating-point math on integer hardware

**Statement**: OpenAI's CIM patent answers a question most readers did not know to ask: how do you sum thousands of floating-point products with adders that only understand integers — by shifting every mantissa once against the block's largest exponent and never converting again until the end.

**Framing**: technical-impossibility walkthrough — the reader's "FP math needs FP hardware" intuition is the door; the alignment trick is the room.

**Evidence required**:
- The mantissa-alignment mechanism end to end
- The integer-domain accumulation and single dequantization
- A baseline of how FP accumulation is normally done

**Evidence available in invention-summary**:
- ✓ Mechanism spans (q-0011-1/2/3, q-0104-1, q-0199-1)
- ✓ Quantitative outputs (q-0145-1, q-0147-1)
- ✗ External baseline for "how GPU FP accumulation normally works" at the depth this framing needs (patent characterizes conventional practice at `[0005]`/`[0133]`, but a rigorous FP-adder baseline would need extra tier-2 sourcing)

**Structural tension**: intuition says floating point requires dedicated floating-point units; the patent claims a structure where the FP part ends at the multiply and everything after is integer plumbing.

**Risks**:
- For the retail-investor reader, the entry point is mantissa/exponent anatomy — heavier lift in ¶1 than Candidate 1's date collision.
- The "impossibility" is soft: any engineer knows alignment makes it possible (every FP adder aligns internally); the hook survives only for lay readers and can read as a strawman to informed ones.
- Standalone, it drops the OpenAI storyline the reader came for (reader-profile: they open for the company, stay for the mechanism).

**Grounding (4-axis — draft)**:
- Claims anchor: 청구항 1 (b)/(c) via `[0011]` (same as Candidate 1)
- Problem anchor: `[0005]` conversion-step cost span
- Effect anchor: `[0104]` "This alignment can ensure that all contributions are represented on a consistent scale..." + `[0147]`
- Baseline-difference anchor: partially external — conventional per-stage conversion (`[0005]`) is patent-internal; independent FP-accumulation baseline not yet sourced (3.5/4)

**Q7 hook pattern (draft)**:
- technical-impossibility
- anchor: reader's "how can integer adders do floating-point math?" → resolved by claim-1 alignment-by-shifting + integer adder tree

**Adversarial defense (draft)**:
- Strongest objection: mantissa alignment before addition is what every floating-point adder already does internally; block-level alignment is textbook block floating point, so the framing "impossible made possible" overstates novelty.
- Mitigation: would need a definitional section separating per-add alignment (standard) from once-per-32-block alignment inside a memory macro (claimed).

**Rejection reason**: Axis 4 external baseline is the weakest of the three (3.5/4 without additional tier-2 research), the hook underserves the default retail-investor reader relative to Candidate 1, and its strongest objection ("this is textbook") attacks the frame itself rather than being containable in one beat. The mechanism survives as the tech core of Candidate 1's sections 2-3; do not resurrect it as the spine.
