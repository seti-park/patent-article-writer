**(EN)** LADDER HOIST ASSEMBLY

요약서
**(EN)** A ladder hoist assembly for transporting materials includes a carriage, a pulley, and a gearbox. The carriage is movably attached to a first major side of a ladder and is configured to carry the materials. The pulley is attached to a top portion of the ladder. The gearbox is attached to a second major side of the ladder that is opposite to the first major side of the ladder. The gearbox is connected to the carriage through a rope routing at least partially on the pulley. The gearbox is configured to wind or unwind the rope to move the carriage along the first major side of the ladder for transporting the materials. In some examples, the gearbox can be remotely controlled. In some examples, the ladder hoist assembly can include a tray mounted to the carriage for carrying items.

TECHNICAL FIELD

[0002] The present disclosure relates to systems and methods for moving panels and other materials. More particularly, some embodiments of the present disclosure relate to hoisting assemblies and mechanisms for moving photovoltaic panels and/or other materials along standard extension ladders,

BACKGROUND

[0003] Existing solutions for moving materials toward or away from fixed structures (e.g., buildings) often rely on custom equipment or cumbersome setup, leading to extended setup times and increased complexity. Additionally, manual efforts are often involved for moving material once setup is completed, which not only increases the chance of damaging the material but may result in injuries or damage to property. It may be desirable to provide hoisting assemblies and mechanisms that can be easily set up using commonly available equipment and/or operated with less manual effort.

DETAWED DESCRIPTION

[0037] Generally described, one or more aspects of the present disclosure relate to hoisting assemblies and mechanisms for moving materials (e.g,, panels, frames, objects, goods, or any other materials) toward, away from, and/or within fixed structures (e.g., moving photovoltaic panels upward to the roofs of residential homes) using standard equipment. More specifically, some embodiments of the present disclosure disclose hoisting mechanisms and assemblies that utilize standard extension ladders to move, transport, or carry materials to and from roofs of buildings with reduced or minimal human labor. In some embodiments, a ladder hoist assembly can include a gearbox, a carriage, and a pulley that can be quickly setup and can be driven by a drill to move photovoltaic panels and/or other materials with little or no involvement of human labor during transportation of the materials.

[0038] Advantageously, through the use of standard equipment (e.g., standard extension ladders, standard drill, and/or off-the-shelf components associated with the gearbox, the carriage, and the pulley) rather than custom equipment (e.g., custom ladder, batteries, motors, or the like), the ladder hoist assembly can be readily available and/or setup in much less time (e.g., within five minutes) compared with existing solutions that may require over half an hour to setup. Through drill-powered operation, little or no manual effort is required to operate the ladder hoist assembly for moving materials, which reduces risk of injuries associated with manually lifting heavy materials. Additionally, various designs or mechanisms (e.g., locking parts, foldable side rollers, or the like) associated with the ladder hoist assemblycan further enhance the safety and robustness associated with the ladder hoist assembly to effectively move materials along standard extension ladders.

[0039] Typically, components or assemblies for moving materials toward or away from fixed structures (e.g., buildings) rely on custom equipment that can take a significant amount of time to setup. For example, existing assemblies for lifting materials to the roofs of buildings utilize custom ladders, batteries, and/or motors such that the assemblies can be complicated and costly to setup or install. Additionally, manual efforts are often involved for moving the materials (e.g., tying ropes around panels and manually pulling the ropes) under existing solutions, which not only increases the chance of damaging the materials but can also result in injuries.

[0040] To address at least a portion of the above problems, a ladder hoist assembly is disclosed for moving materials or goods (e.g,, panels, frames, or the like) toward, away from, and/or within fixed structures (e.g,, moving photovoltaic panels upward to the roofs of residential homes) using standard equipment m accordance with some embodiments of the present disclosure. In some embodiments, the ladder hoist assembly utilizes a standard extension ladder to move, transport, or carry materials to and from the roofs of buildings with reduced or minimized human labor. The ladder hoist assembly can include a carriage, a pulley, and a gearbox that are attached to the standard extension ladder.

[0041] In some implementations, the gearbox is attached to the back of the extension ladder and, when powered by a standard drill, spins a spool within the gearbox to move a rope. The rope routes to the top of the standard extension ladder through the pulley and back down to the front of the standard extension ladder, where the rope connects the gearbox to the carriage. This configuration allows the carriage carrying materials (e.g., photovoltaic panels) to move up and down along the standard extension ladder, effectively transporting the photovoltaic panels and/or other materials to and from the roof with reduced or minimal manual effort. Advantageously, the design of the ladder hoist assembly enables quick setup (e.g., within five minutes) and leverages standard, readily available, and/or off-the-shelf components, making the ladder hoist assembly both time-efficient and cost-effective to setup and operate.

For example, the carriage 102 is movably atached to a first major side (e.g., a front side) of the ladder 150.

[0044] In some embodiments, the carriage 102 is designed to carry materials such as photovoltaic (PV) panels, boxes, and/or other items. As will be described with greater detail with reference to FIGS. 2 and 3, the carriage 102 can include sliding plates and sliding rollers that allow the carnage 102 to move or slide along the front side of the ladder 150. Additionally and/or optionally, the carriage 102 can include securing components (e.g., cords 204 and brackets 206) to secure the materials to the carriage 102.

[0045] In some embodiments, the gearbox 104 is attached to a second major side (e.g., a back side) of the ladder 150, where the second major side is opposite to the first major side of the ladder 150. The gearbox 104 can be a worm-driven gearbox (e.g., 10: 1 worm-driven gearbox, 5:1 worm-driven gearbox, 15:1 w’orm-driven gearbox, or the like) or other types of gearboxes in certain embodiments. The gearbox 104 is configured to transfer torque from adrill (not shown in FIG. 1 A) to rotate a spool within the gearbox 104. The rotating spool winds or unwinds a rope 180 to move the carriage 102 along the first major side of the ladder 150 to transport the materials (e.g., from a ground to a roof of a building). For example, the gearbox 104 can be a 10:1 worm-driven gearbox in which every ten rotations of the drill result in one rotation of the spool. By selecting a proper ratio (e.g., 10:1) for the gearbox 104, the ladder hoist assembly 100 can be reliably operated. The gearbox 104 can be connected to the carriage 102 through the rope 180 that routes at least partially on or through a pulley 106 (shown in FIG. IB).

[0046] As noted above, the rope 180 can be routed from the gearbox 104, up to the top of the ladder 150, and back down to the front of the ladder 150. The rope 180 can connect the carriage 102 with the gearbox 104. The rope 180 can be made of various materials (e.g., nylon, metal, or the like) to handle the weight of the panels and/or items being transported, ensuring safe and reliable operation of the ladder hoist assembly 100, The rope 180 can transmit mechanical force generated by the gearbox 104 to the carriage 102, enabling the movement of the carriage 102 to cany panels or items along the ladder 150. The ladder 150 can be a standard extension ladder that serves as the structural basis for the ladder hoist assembly 100 to be attached, mounted, and/or secured to.

[0047] As shown in FIG. IB, the pulley 106 can be a component of the ladder hoist assembly 100 that is mounted to a top portion of the ladder 150. In some embodiments, the pulley 106 can guide the rope 180 connecting the gearbox 104 and the carriage 102 to wind or unwind, facilitating the movement of materials earned by the carriage 102 along the ladder 150. The pulley 106 can ensure that the rope 180 follows a controlled path, reducing friction and wear on the rope 180, and/or providing smooth operation of the ladder hoist assembly 100. As will be described with greater detail with reference to FIG. 5, the pulley 106 can include at least a wheel with a grooved rim, which allows the rope 180 to route securely within the groove, thereby reducing the risk of the rope 180 slipping off the pulley 106 during operation.

a carriage movably attached to a first major side of a ladder, the carriage configured to carry the materials;

a pulley attached to a top portion of the ladder; and

a gearbox attached to a second major side of the ladder that is opposite to the first major side of the ladder, the gearbox connected to the carriage through a rope routing at least partially on the pulley,

wherein the gearbox is configured to wind or unwind the rope to move the carriage along the first major side of the ladder for transporting the materials.

2. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the pulley comprises a wheel with a grooved rim on which the rope routes,

3. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the pulley comprises a first bracket configured to mount the pulley to a first rung of the ladder.

4. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 3, wherein the pulley comprises a second bracket configured to lock the pulley to a second rung of the ladder, and wherein the second rung is below the first rung.

5. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the pulley comprises a U-bolt configured to prevent the carriage from moving over the ladder.

6. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 5, wherein the rope routes through the LT-bolt.

7. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the carriage comprises a plurality of sliding rollers configured to enable the carriage to move along the first major side of the ladder.

8. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the ladder is a standard extension ladder.

9. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 8, wherein the carriage comprises a plurality of sliding plates configured to provide a plurality of ramps to enable the carriage to move smoothly around an extension portion of the standard extension ladder.

10. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the carriage comprises a first side roller and a second side roller configured to engage a first minor side of the ladder and a second minor side of the ladder.11. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 10, wherein the first side roller and the second side roller are foldable.

11. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the carriage comprises a cord and a plastic bracket configured to secure the materials.

12. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the materials comprise a photovoltaic panel.

13. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the gearbox comprises a rung bracket configured to secure the gearbox to a rung of the ladder,

14. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the gearbox comprises a 5:1 worm-driven gearbox, a 10: 1 worm-driven gearbox, or a 15:1 worm-driven gearbox.

15. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, wherein the gearbox comprises a spool, and wherein the gearbox transfers a torque from a drill to rotate the spool to wind or unwind the rope.

16. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 16, wherein the gearbox comprises a drill adapter configured to (i) transmit the torque from the drill to the gearbox and (u) adapt a chuck of the drill to an input shaft of the gearbox.

17. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 16, wherein the gearbox comprises a remote control assembly configured to enable remote controlling the gearbox to rotate the spool to wind or unwind the rope.

18. The ladder hoist assembly of claim 1, further comprising:

a tray mounted to the carriage, wherein the tray structurally forms a slot to accommodate a conduit for transporting the conduit.

20. A kit for converting an extension ladder to use for transporting materials up to a roof, the extension ladder comprising a base section and at least one fly section, the kit comprising:

a carriage configured to slide along rails of the base section and rails of the fly section;

a pulley configured to be supported by the extension ladder;

a gearbox configured to be supported by the extension ladder and comprising a hand drill adapter; anda rope or cable configured to be routed from the gearbox, through the pulley, to the carriage,

wherein rotation of the hand drill adapter causes the carriage to slide along the rails of the base section and the rails of the fly section.
