## discovery

- **title**: Etched Put Its No-Switch Memory Idea in Writing in May 2023
- **lead**: Two years before Etched pitched "the best memory layer is no layer" on stage, both co-founders had signed that idea into the company's very first patent filing. US 2024/0378175 A1, filed 10 May 2023, asks for HBM channels wired straight into the columns of a giant multi-chip math array, with no switch in between. The stage version arrived in July 2026 as shipping hardware, by the company's telling. The written version is three years older, and the patent office still hasn't said yes to it.
- **rationale**: This is the reader_sentence delivered in ¶1's order: the unknown, checkable fact first (the idea in writing, May 2023, two years early), the "hasn't said yes yet" pricing last.

## tension

- **title**: On Stage It Ships. At the Patent Office It Is Still Being Argued
- **lead**: In July 2026 Etched told the world its no-layer memory design was shipping in racks. The USPTO file on that same idea reads differently: claims filed in May 2023, argued with an examiner ever since, and the company still paying to keep the argument alive. Both records are real, and they describe one architecture at two altitudes: a product story on stage, and the unfinished price of trying to own the idea behind it.
- **rationale**: The two halves of the reader_sentence (wrote it early / no yes yet) become the ¶1 collision itself, so the insurance facts ARE the beat rather than queueing before it.

## contrarian

- **title**: Etched's Memory Story Has a Paper Trail. It Starts in May 2023
- **lead**: The published read of Etched's patent record said the memory half of its architecture was missing: the granted wiring patents never wrote it down. Open the company's very first filing and the picture inverts. The no-switch memory idea is there in claim language, dated 10 May 2023, signed by both co-founders, two years before the hype thread. What it is not, yet, is granted.
- **rationale**: States the citable consensus (the companion essay's "memory half absent from the granted record") in one sentence, then inverts it with the reader_sentence's discovery; the pricing clause lands last.

## insider

- **title**: Reading Etched's First Patent Filing, the One the Thread Echoes
- **lead**: Before Etched had a product name, a rack, or a hype thread, it had application US 18/195,769: both co-founders on the title page, chips stitched into one giant math array, and memory wired straight into its columns with no switch in the way. Almost everyone arguing about this company is arguing about the July 2026 thread. The document underneath it is three years older, still being argued at the patent office, and it says things the thread does not.
- **rationale**: Privileged-view framing turns the reader_sentence into something the reader saw for themselves by opening the origin document over the essayist's shoulder.

## stakes

- **title**: Etched's Whole Patent Stack Is Loan Collateral. This One Included
- **lead**: When Etched borrowed from TriplePoint Capital, what it pledged was its patent stack: first the four applications it had in early 2024, this origin filing among them, then a 2025 pool that included its newly granted patents. That is the investor-grade fact about these documents: they are the company's bankable asset class, and what a creditor reaches if the bet fails. The oldest paper in the pledged stack is the May 2023 filing where both co-founders wrote down the no-switch memory idea the company now pitches on stage, an idea the patent office has yet to allow.
- **rationale**: Leads with the money fact (portfolio-scope, symmetric) and lets the reader_sentence price what is actually in the vault; collateral stays blanket-over-portfolio, never this-application-specific.
