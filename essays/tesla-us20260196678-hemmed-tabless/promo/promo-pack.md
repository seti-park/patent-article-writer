## 2. English thread (2-4 message-unit posts)

```text
On today's tabless lines, lasers slit the bare foil edge into flags before winding. Tesla made that manufacturing-simplification story famous.

Its next electrode filing does something else. It does not cut the foil into flags. It folds the edge, and the fold is the tab.
```

```text
The geometry is a hem. The bare foil edge turns back on itself past the electrode film, like a trouser hem. Wound into a jellyroll, one winding of that hemmed edge touches the next, and the collector sits on the folded rim as both electrical contact and mechanical attachment.

The filing's sharpest paragraph is not a claim chart. It is an equipment list: a hemmed line may not necessarily require a tabbing device, slitting device, notching device, flag-interleaving device, or debris-handling device. Neither add a tab nor subtract a flag. Only fold.

Reading that list as a critique of cut-edge practice is the essay's mapping. The inference stands on the document's own words. The filing never names the 4680 or any production line.
```
