# Tesla Made Tabless Famous. Its New Patent Deletes the Machines

![FIG. 2: wound hems as the cell's terminal](input/figures/fig-02.png)

*FIG. 2: device cross-section. Successive windings of the hemmed edge (230-1, 230-2) contact each other at the wound end; the collector attaches to that folded rim inside the can (250).*

## Battery Day Sold Tabless. This Filing Deletes the Cutting Line

On today's tabless lines, lasers slit the bare foil edge into flags before winding. Tesla's new electrode filing lists tabbing, slitting, notching, flag-interleaving, and debris-handling devices as machines a hemmed line "may not necessarily require" [0025]. The replacement for all five is a fold: the foil's bare edge turned back on itself, becoming the terminal the tab used to be.

**Tesla's next tabless filing doesn't cut the foil into flags. It folds the edge, and the fold is the tab.**

That is the geometry on paper. The stack of cathode, anode, and separators is wound into a jellyroll. In that roll, one winding of the hemmed edge touches the next, and the collector attaches to the folded rim.

US 2026/0196678 A1, "Hemmed Tabless Electrodes for Energy Storage Devices, and Methods Thereof," is a pending Tesla application filed 6 January 2025 and published 9 July 2026. Every manufacturing and performance upside in the document is a "can" or "may" assertion. The fold itself is already claimed as sought scope: the protection Tesla is asking for, not yet granted.

## Attach a Tab, Cut Flags, or Fold the Edge

Jellyroll cells connect their electrodes to exterior terminals through electrode tabs [0002]. Forming those tabs "can increase manufacturing costs and present manufacturing challenges" [0002]. Because the tabs are additional components, "they add additional thickness to the device and must themselves be rolled into the wound electrode" [0002]. The Background names only that problem. It is the attach-a-part path.

Industry tabless practice took a different cut. On current tabless cylindrical lines, the bare foil edge is laser-cut into flags, and material is removed before the cell collects current along the whole edge.

This filing does neither. An electrode film sits on a foil that keeps an exposed bare edge at one end [0027] [0029]. That edge is hemmed, folded back on itself like the hem of a trouser leg, until "the hemmed edge extends beyond a top edge of the electrode film" [0005]. The foil still has a core edge, a can edge, and a hemmed edge between them [0005].

No tab is welded on. Nothing is cut away. The method claim's skeleton is two steps: provide an electrode film on a foil, and hem an edge of the foil to form a hemmed electrode [0011].

*FIG. 1: layered stack mid-wind. Foils (102, 106) carry films (110, 120); the exposed foil edge folds into hems (116, 118) that protrude past the film before the jellyroll is finished.*

Three paths to make a connection, then. Attach a tab. Cut flags. Or fold a hem. This filing takes the third.

## The Fold Is the Terminal, Winding Against Winding

Once the hemmed electrodes and separators are wound around the central axis, the architecture is simple. Independent claim 1 seeks a wound assembly in which a proceeding winding of the hemmed edge contacts a succeeding winding, and a collector sits on that folded rim as both electrical contact and mechanical attachment:

> "a proceeding winding of the hemmed edge contacts a succeeding winding of the hemmed edge"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0004]

> "the collector is in electrical communication and mechanically attached to the hemmed edge"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0004]

The description matches the claim: the hems "may be compressed such that each hem is in contact with the hem adjacent to it" [0019], and the hems are there "for making an electrical connection to the cell terminals" [0019].

Is the cell tabbed or tabless? Function is the tab's replacement. Category is tabless. Shape is a continuous folded rim, winding contacting winding, not a local tongue welded at one spot.

The drawings show the geometry as teardrop loops at successive winding positions, with collectors, insulator, and lid closing the can around the wound assembly [0035] [0036] [0037]. The document says the hemmed electrode can improve internal electrical resistance "by minimizing geometric bottlenecks (e.g., electron travel distance) from cell terminals and electrochemically-active material sites throughout the electrode assembly" [0025]. That is a can-claim. No resistance number appears in the document.

## The Equipment List Reads Like a Flag-Line Invoice

Production tabless lines cut the bare foil edge into flags. The trade press describes the practice directly: "the tab extends along the entire battery foil, with a slit cut between each flag," and a tabless design increases the total number of cuts. Equipment vendors sell 46-series laser notching and winding machines as a product category for those lines. Samsung SDI has announced 46-series cylindrical cells on a tabless design. The category is no longer Tesla-only.

Against that baseline, the filing's sharpest paragraph is not a claim chart. It is an equipment list:

> "the machine for making a hemmed electrode may not necessarily require the use of a tabbing device, slitting device, notching device, flag interleaving device, or debris handling device to eliminate foil near the core and/or can edges, and/or exhaust systems for all of these devices"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0025]

The filing never names the 4680, Tesla's own 2019 tabless patent, or any production line. Its Background attacks only tabs [0002]. Reading the device list as a critique of the industry's cut-edge practice is still the essay's mapping. The inference stands on the document's own words.

The names map onto production jobs. A tabbing device belongs to the old attach-a-tab line. A flag interleaving device is flag-line specific; it has no job on a tab-only line. Slitting and notching also serve other jobs on other lines. The patent's purpose clause says the five devices are not necessarily required to eliminate foil near the core and can edges. Debris handling rides with the cutting. A "flag interleaving device" cannot refer to a tab welder.

The document also says "the hems do not require slits to form the hemmed electrode" [0042], which can "increase the structural integrity of the hemmed electrode and mitigate bending and tearing damage under tensile loads" [0042]. And "the wound hemmed electrode assembly does not include any additional geometries associated with the addition of foil material (e.g. “tabs”) or the subtraction of foil material (e.g. “flags”)" [0038]. Neither add a tab nor subtract a flag. Only fold. The hemmed electrode "can reduce overall costs and solve many manufacturing challenges" [0025], again a can, not a measured saving.

*FIG. 5: method steps 510 to 560. Provide, hem, separator, wind, connect collector, insert into housing. No slitting, notching, or flag step in the box flow.*

Tesla's first tabless patent, filed in 2019 and granted in 2023 as US 11,749,842 B2, described a continuous exposed conductive edge formed by selective coating, not by cutting the foil. Production practice on tabless cylindrical cells still cuts flags. This 2025 filing prices the machine bill of that cut-edge line. Whether Tesla swaps its production process is outside the document. The filing reads as a critique of cutting. It does not say the company has abandoned it.

## The Claims Seek a Fold. The Description Offers a Menu of Shapes

Three independent claims seek the architecture: one for the wound assembly, one for the electrode, one for the method. Claim 1 seeks the wound hemmed assembly with winding-to-winding hem contact and a collector electrically and mechanically attached to the hemmed edge [0004]. Claim 2 seeks an electrode whose foil has a core edge, a can edge, and a hemmed edge between them, with that hemmed edge extending beyond the film's top edge [0005]. Claim 13 seeks a method of providing the film-on-foil electrode and hemming an edge of the foil [0011]. All three are sought scope on a pending application: protection asked for, not yet granted. None pins a numerical dimension.

The description is freer. The document says "the hem may have a teardrop shape in the hemmed electrode prior to winding" [0021]. It "may be connected to the collectors by forced contact, solder joint, brazing, ultrasonic welding, laser welding, adhering, and combinations thereof" [0023]. Film-to-hem separation length, and loop height and width, appear as design menus, not claim pins [0038]. Flattening the hemmed edges "can create a substantially uniform (e.g. flat and void-free) interface for current collector joining" [0047], still an option, not a requirement of the independent claims.

*FIG. 3: two-loop hem variant, both loops bent toward the can side (330-1, 330-2). Shape is description menu, not claim lock.*

*FIG. 4: same two-loop idea, second loop bent back toward the core side (430-1, 430-2). Bend direction alone is the delta.*

What is claimed is that geometry. What is described is a kit of shapes and joining methods around it. Confusing the two would turn options into promises.

## Watch Grant, Continuations, and the Line Itself

Tesla made tabless famous as a manufacturing-simplification story. This next filing keeps the tabless category and replaces cutting with folding. Its own equipment list reads as the bill of machines a flag-cutting line would no longer need. The geometry is sought in three independent claims. The upsides are all can and may.

If these claims are granted in something close to the sought form, that would lock the claimed scope, no more. Two things would carry the mapping: continuation filings that keep the hem-without-slits core, and production-line changes that drop the cutting stack the list names. Companies announce tabless when it is convenient. They file when the edge is already being redesigned on paper.

단자와 잇기 위해 전극 탭(tab)을 쓴다 `[0002]`. 이 문서가 출발점으로 삼는 문제는 그 탭
자체다. 전극에 탭을 형성하는 일이 제조 비용을 올리고 제조상 난제를 만들며 `[0002]`, 탭은
추가 부품이어서 장치에 두께를 더하고 감긴 전극 안으로 함께 말려 들어가야 한다 `[0002]`.

## ② 기존 기술의 해결 방향과 그 한계

종래 해법은 포일에 무언가를 붙이는 것이었다. 전극 탭이 전극과 하우징 외부 단자를
전기적으로 잇지만 `[0002]`, 위 ①의 비용·두께·권취 부담이 그대로 따라온다. 명세서는 또
다른 방향, 즉 포일을 잘라내 접속부(flag)를 만드는 무탭 방식을 전제로 한 장비군을
나열한다. 슬리팅·노칭·flag interleaving·부스러기 처리 장치와 그 배기 설비가 그것이고,
헴 방식은 이런 장비가 필요 없을 수 있다고 대비한다 `[0025]`. 또 헴은 슬릿이 필요 없어서,
슬릿을 쓰는 방식이 감수하는 인장 하중에서의 굽힘·찢김 손상 문제를 피한다고 서술한다
`[0042]`. 다만 본문에 인용 선행문헌 목록은 없고, 이 대비는 명세서 자체 서술이다.

- `[0025]`: "the machine for making a hemmed electrode may not necessarily require the use of a tabbing device, slitting device, notching device, flag interleaving device, or debris handling device"
  (헴 전극 제조기는 태빙·슬리팅·노칭·플래그 삽입·부스러기 처리 장치가 필요 없을 수 있다는 뜻)
- `[0042]`: "the hems do not require slits to form the hemmed electrode"
  (헴 전극을 만드는 데에는 슬릿이 필요 없다는 뜻)

## ③ 이 특허의 독창적 해법 (핵심 청구항 중심)

독립항은 셋이다. 장치(1항), 전극(2항), 방법(13항). 출원 건이므로 locked 는 없고, 아래
필수 요소는 전부 sought-locked(청구 중, 확정 아님)다.

- **1항(장치)**: 헴 전극과 분리막을 감은 조립체가 하우징 안에 있고, 헴 가장자리의 앞
  감김(proceeding winding)과 뒤 감김(succeeding winding)이 서로 접촉하며 `[0004]`,
  컬렉터(collector)가 그 헴 가장자리에 전기적으로 통하고 기계적으로 붙어 있을 것을
  요구한다 `[0004]`.
- **2항(전극)**: 필름이 포일 위에 있고, 포일에 core edge · can edge · 그 사이의 hemmed
  edge 가 있으며, 헴 가장자리가 전극 필름 상단보다 위로 나와 있을 것을 요구한다 `[0005]`.

# Tesla Made Tabless Famous. Its New Patent Deletes the Machines

## Battery Day Sold Tabless. This Filing Deletes the Cutting Line

On today's tabless lines, lasers slit the bare foil edge into flags before winding. Tesla's new electrode filing lists tabbing, slitting, notching, flag-interleaving, and debris-handling devices as machines a hemmed line "may not necessarily require" [0025]. The replacement for all five is a fold: the foil's bare edge turned back on itself, becoming the terminal the tab used to be.

**Tesla's next tabless filing doesn't cut the foil into flags. It folds the edge, and the fold is the tab.**

That is the geometry on paper. The stack of cathode, anode, and separators is wound into a jellyroll. In that roll, one winding of the hemmed edge touches the next, and the collector attaches to the folded rim.

US 2026/0196678 A1, "Hemmed Tabless Electrodes for Energy Storage Devices, and Methods Thereof," is a pending Tesla application filed 6 January 2025 and published 9 July 2026. Every manufacturing and performance upside in the document is a "can" or "may" assertion. The fold itself is already claimed as sought scope: the protection Tesla is asking for, not yet granted.

## Attach a Tab, Cut Flags, or Fold the Edge

Jellyroll cells connect their electrodes to exterior terminals through electrode tabs [0002]. Forming those tabs "can increase manufacturing costs and present manufacturing challenges" [0002]. Because the tabs are additional components, "they add additional thickness to the device and must themselves be rolled into the wound electrode" [0002]. The Background names only that problem. It is the attach-a-part path.

Industry tabless practice took a different cut. On current tabless cylindrical lines, the bare foil edge is laser-cut into flags, and material is removed before the cell collects current along the whole edge.

This filing does neither. An electrode film sits on a foil that keeps an exposed bare edge at one end [0027] [0029]. That edge is hemmed, folded back on itself like the hem of a trouser leg, until "the hemmed edge extends beyond a top edge of the electrode film" [0005]. The foil still has a core edge, a can edge, and a hemmed edge between them [0005].

No tab is welded on. Nothing is cut away. The method claim's skeleton is two steps: provide an electrode film on a foil, and hem an edge of the foil to form a hemmed electrode [0011].

*FIG. 1: layered stack mid-wind. Foils (102, 106) carry films (110, 120); the exposed foil edge folds into hems (116, 118) that protrude past the film before the jellyroll is finished.*

Three paths to make a connection, then. Attach a tab. Cut flags. Or fold a hem. This filing takes the third.

## The Fold Is the Terminal, Winding Against Winding

Once the hemmed electrodes and separators are wound around the central axis, the architecture is simple. Independent claim 1 seeks a wound assembly in which a proceeding winding of the hemmed edge contacts a succeeding winding, and a collector sits on that folded rim as both electrical contact and mechanical attachment:

> "a proceeding winding of the hemmed edge contacts a succeeding winding of the hemmed edge"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0004]

> "the collector is in electrical communication and mechanically attached to the hemmed edge"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0004]

The description matches the claim: the hems "may be compressed such that each hem is in contact with the hem adjacent to it" [0019], and the hems are there "for making an electrical connection to the cell terminals" [0019].

Is the cell tabbed or tabless? Function is the tab's replacement. Category is tabless. Shape is a continuous folded rim, winding contacting winding, not a local tongue welded at one spot.

The drawings show the geometry as teardrop loops at successive winding positions, with collectors, insulator, and lid closing the can around the wound assembly [0035] [0036] [0037]. The document says the hemmed electrode can improve internal electrical resistance "by minimizing geometric bottlenecks (e.g., electron travel distance) from cell terminals and electrochemically-active material sites throughout the electrode assembly" [0025]. That is a can-claim. No resistance number appears in the document.

## The Equipment List Reads Like a Flag-Line Invoice

Production tabless lines cut the bare foil edge into flags. The trade press describes the practice directly: "the tab extends along the entire battery foil, with a slit cut between each flag," and a tabless design increases the total number of cuts. Equipment vendors sell 46-series laser notching and winding machines as a product category for those lines. Samsung SDI has announced 46-series cylindrical cells on a tabless design. The category is no longer Tesla-only.

Against that baseline, the filing's sharpest paragraph is not a claim chart. It is an equipment list:

> "the machine for making a hemmed electrode may not necessarily require the use of a tabbing device, slitting device, notching device, flag interleaving device, or debris handling device to eliminate foil near the core and/or can edges, and/or exhaust systems for all of these devices"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0025]

The filing never names the 4680, Tesla's own 2019 tabless patent, or any production line. Its Background attacks only tabs [0002]. Reading the device list as a critique of the industry's cut-edge practice is still the essay's mapping. The inference stands on the document's own words.

The names map onto production jobs. A tabbing device belongs to the old attach-a-tab line. A flag interleaving device is flag-line specific; it has no job on a tab-only line. Slitting and notching also serve other jobs on other lines. The patent's purpose clause says the five devices are not necessarily required to eliminate foil near the core and can edges. Debris handling rides with the cutting. A "flag interleaving device" cannot refer to a tab welder.

The document also says "the hems do not require slits to form the hemmed electrode" [0042], which can "increase the structural integrity of the hemmed electrode and mitigate bending and tearing damage under tensile loads" [0042]. And "the wound hemmed electrode assembly does not include any additional geometries associated with the addition of foil material (e.g. “tabs”) or the subtraction of foil material (e.g. “flags”)" [0038]. Neither add a tab nor subtract a flag. Only fold. The hemmed electrode "can reduce overall costs and solve many manufacturing challenges" [0025], again a can, not a measured saving.

*FIG. 5: method steps 510 to 560. Provide, hem, separator, wind, connect collector, insert into housing. No slitting, notching, or flag step in the box flow.*

Tesla's first tabless patent, filed in 2019 and granted in 2023 as US 11,749,842 B2, described a continuous exposed conductive edge formed by selective coating, not by cutting the foil. Production practice on tabless cylindrical cells still cuts flags. This 2025 filing prices the machine bill of that cut-edge line. Whether Tesla swaps its production process is outside the document. The filing reads as a critique of cutting. It does not say the company has abandoned it.

## The Claims Seek a Fold. The Description Offers a Menu of Shapes

Three independent claims seek the architecture: one for the wound assembly, one for the electrode, one for the method. Claim 1 seeks the wound hemmed assembly with winding-to-winding hem contact and a collector electrically and mechanically attached to the hemmed edge [0004]. Claim 2 seeks an electrode whose foil has a core edge, a can edge, and a hemmed edge between them, with that hemmed edge extending beyond the film's top edge [0005]. Claim 13 seeks a method of providing the film-on-foil electrode and hemming an edge of the foil [0011]. All three are sought scope on a pending application: protection asked for, not yet granted. None pins a numerical dimension.

The description is freer. The document says "the hem may have a teardrop shape in the hemmed electrode prior to winding" [0021]. It "may be connected to the collectors by forced contact, solder joint, brazing, ultrasonic welding, laser welding, adhering, and combinations thereof" [0023]. Film-to-hem separation length, and loop height and width, appear as design menus, not claim pins [0038]. Flattening the hemmed edges "can create a substantially uniform (e.g. flat and void-free) interface for current collector joining" [0047], still an option, not a requirement of the independent claims.

*FIG. 3: two-loop hem variant, both loops bent toward the can side (330-1, 330-2). Shape is description menu, not claim lock.*

*FIG. 4: same two-loop idea, second loop bent back toward the core side (430-1, 430-2). Bend direction alone is the delta.*

What is claimed is that geometry. What is described is a kit of shapes and joining methods around it. Confusing the two would turn options into promises.

## Watch Grant, Continuations, and the Line Itself

Tesla made tabless famous as a manufacturing-simplification story. This next filing keeps the tabless category and replaces cutting with folding. Its own equipment list reads as the bill of machines a flag-cutting line would no longer need. The geometry is sought in three independent claims. The upsides are all can and may.

If these claims are granted in something close to the sought form, that would lock the claimed scope, no more. Two things would carry the mapping: continuation filings that keep the hem-without-slits core, and production-line changes that drop the cutting stack the list names. Companies announce tabless when it is convenient. They file when the edge is already being redesigned on paper.

Is the cell tabbed or tabless? Function is the tab's replacement. Category is tabless. Shape is a continuous folded rim, winding contacting winding, not a local tongue welded at one spot.

The drawings show the geometry as teardrop loops at successive winding positions, with collectors, insulator, and lid closing the can around the wound assembly [0035] [0036] [0037]. The document says the hemmed electrode can improve internal electrical resistance "by minimizing geometric bottlenecks (e.g., electron travel distance) from cell terminals and electrochemically-active material sites throughout the electrode assembly" [0025]. That is a can-claim. No resistance number appears in the document.

## The Equipment List Reads Like a Flag-Line Invoice

Production tabless lines cut the bare foil edge into flags. The trade press describes the practice directly: "the tab extends along the entire battery foil, with a slit cut between each flag," and a tabless design increases the total number of cuts. Equipment vendors sell 46-series laser notching and winding machines as a product category for those lines. Samsung SDI has announced 46-series cylindrical cells on a tabless design. The category is no longer Tesla-only.

Against that baseline, the filing's sharpest paragraph is not a claim chart. It is an equipment list:

> "the machine for making a hemmed electrode may not necessarily require the use of a tabbing device, slitting device, notching device, flag interleaving device, or debris handling device to eliminate foil near the core and/or can edges, and/or exhaust systems for all of these devices"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0025]

The filing never names the 4680, Tesla's own 2019 tabless patent, or any production line. Its Background attacks only tabs [0002]. Reading the device list as a critique of the industry's cut-edge practice is still the essay's mapping. The inference stands on the document's own words.

The names map onto production jobs. A tabbing device belongs to the old attach-a-tab line. A flag interleaving device is flag-line specific; it has no job on a tab-only line. Slitting and notching also serve other jobs on other lines. The patent's purpose clause says the five devices are not necessarily required to eliminate foil near the core and can edges. Debris handling rides with the cutting. A "flag interleaving device" cannot refer to a tab welder.

The document also says "the hems do not require slits to form the hemmed electrode" [0042], which can "increase the structural integrity of the hemmed electrode and mitigate bending and tearing damage under tensile loads" [0042]. And "the wound hemmed electrode assembly does not include any additional geometries associated with the addition of foil material (e.g. “tabs”) or the subtraction of foil material (e.g. “flags”)" [0038]. Neither add a tab nor subtract a flag. Only fold. The hemmed electrode "can reduce overall costs and solve many manufacturing challenges" [0025], again a can, not a measured saving.

*FIG. 5: method steps 510 to 560. Provide, hem, separator, wind, connect collector, insert into housing. No slitting, notching, or flag step in the box flow.*

Tesla's first tabless patent, filed in 2019 and granted in 2023 as US 11,749,842 B2, described a continuous exposed conductive edge formed by selective coating, not by cutting the foil. Production practice on tabless cylindrical cells still cuts flags. This 2025 filing prices the machine bill of that cut-edge line. Whether Tesla swaps its production process is outside the document. The filing reads as a critique of cutting. It does not say the company has abandoned it.

## The Claims Seek a Fold. The Description Offers a Menu of Shapes

Three independent claims seek the architecture: one for the wound assembly, one for the electrode, one for the method. Claim 1 seeks the wound hemmed assembly with winding-to-winding hem contact and a collector electrically and mechanically attached to the hemmed edge [0004]. Claim 2 seeks an electrode whose foil has a core edge, a can edge, and a hemmed edge between them, with that hemmed edge extending beyond the film's top edge [0005]. Claim 13 seeks a method of providing the film-on-foil electrode and hemming an edge of the foil [0011]. All three are sought scope on a pending application: protection asked for, not yet granted. None pins a numerical dimension.

The description is freer. The document says "the hem may have a teardrop shape in the hemmed electrode prior to winding" [0021]. It "may be connected to the collectors by forced contact, solder joint, brazing, ultrasonic welding, laser welding, adhering, and combinations thereof" [0023]. Film-to-hem separation length, and loop height and width, appear as design menus, not claim pins [0038]. Flattening the hemmed edges "can create a substantially uniform (e.g. flat and void-free) interface for current collector joining" [0047], still an option, not a requirement of the independent claims.

*FIG. 3: two-loop hem variant, both loops bent toward the can side (330-1, 330-2). Shape is description menu, not claim lock.*

*FIG. 4: same two-loop idea, second loop bent back toward the core side (430-1, 430-2). Bend direction alone is the delta.*

What is claimed is that geometry. What is described is a kit of shapes and joining methods around it. Confusing the two would turn options into promises.

## Watch Grant, Continuations, and the Line Itself

Tesla made tabless famous as a manufacturing-simplification story. This next filing keeps the tabless category and replaces cutting with folding. Its own equipment list reads as the bill of machines a flag-cutting line would no longer need. The geometry is sought in three independent claims. The upsides are all can and may.

If these claims are granted in something close to the sought form, that would lock the claimed scope, no more. Two things would carry the mapping: continuation filings that keep the hem-without-slits core, and production-line changes that drop the cutting stack the list names. Companies announce tabless when it is convenient. They file when the edge is already being redesigned on paper.

## ④ 기대 효과

전부 명세서 주장("can/may")이며 측정 수치는 없다. 제조 측면에서는 탭·슬릿 계열 장비의
제거로 전체 비용을 줄이고 여러 제조 난제(장비 복잡도·설치 면적·에너지·스크랩·인력,

셀 단자에서 활물질까지의 전자 이동 거리 병목을 최소화해 내부 전기 저항을 개선할 수 있다고
하고 `[0025]`, 안전 측면에서는 열폭주 가스 배출 경로와 내부 여유 공간을 든다 `[0025]`.
부수 효과로, 슬릿 없는 헴의 구조 강성 `[0042]`, 필름과 헴 사이 설계 간격의 열 노출 감소
`[0038]`, 평탄화 시 공극 없는 접합면 `[0047]` 이 서술된다.

**근거 (verbatim):**

# Tesla Made Tabless Famous. Its New Patent Deletes the Machines

![FIG. 2: wound hems as the cell's terminal](input/figures/fig-02.png)

*FIG. 2: device cross-section. Successive windings of the hemmed edge (230-1, 230-2) contact each other at the wound end; the collector attaches to that folded rim inside the can (250).*

## Battery Day Sold Tabless. This Filing Deletes the Cutting Line

On today's tabless lines, lasers slit the bare foil edge into flags before winding. Tesla's new electrode filing lists tabbing, slitting, notching, flag-interleaving, and debris-handling devices as machines a hemmed line "may not necessarily require" [0025]. The replacement for all five is a fold: the foil's bare edge turned back on itself, becoming the terminal the tab used to be.

**Tesla's next tabless filing doesn't cut the foil into flags. It folds the edge, and the fold is the tab.**

That is the geometry on paper. The stack of cathode, anode, and separators is wound into a jellyroll. In that roll, one winding of the hemmed edge touches the next, and the collector attaches to the folded rim.

US 2026/0196678 A1, "Hemmed Tabless Electrodes for Energy Storage Devices, and Methods Thereof," is a pending Tesla application filed 6 January 2025 and published 9 July 2026. Every manufacturing and performance upside in the document is a "can" or "may" assertion. The fold itself is already claimed as sought scope: the protection Tesla is asking for, not yet granted.

## Attach a Tab, Cut Flags, or Fold the Edge

Jellyroll cells connect their electrodes to exterior terminals through electrode tabs [0002]. Forming those tabs "can increase manufacturing costs and present manufacturing challenges" [0002]. Because the tabs are additional components, "they add additional thickness to the device and must themselves be rolled into the wound electrode" [0002]. The Background names only that problem. It is the attach-a-part path.

Industry tabless practice took a different cut. On current tabless cylindrical lines, the bare foil edge is laser-cut into flags, and material is removed before the cell collects current along the whole edge.

This filing does neither. An electrode film sits on a foil that keeps an exposed bare edge at one end [0027] [0029]. That edge is hemmed, folded back on itself like the hem of a trouser leg, until "the hemmed edge extends beyond a top edge of the electrode film" [0005]. The foil still has a core edge, a can edge, and a hemmed edge between them [0005].

No tab is welded on. Nothing is cut away. The method claim's skeleton is two steps: provide an electrode film on a foil, and hem an edge of the foil to form a hemmed electrode [0011].

*FIG. 1: layered stack mid-wind. Foils (102, 106) carry films (110, 120); the exposed foil edge folds into hems (116, 118) that protrude past the film before the jellyroll is finished.*

Three paths to make a connection, then. Attach a tab. Cut flags. Or fold a hem. This filing takes the third.

## The Fold Is the Terminal, Winding Against Winding

Once the hemmed electrodes and separators are wound around the central axis, the architecture is simple. Independent claim 1 seeks a wound assembly in which a proceeding winding of the hemmed edge contacts a succeeding winding, and a collector sits on that folded rim as both electrical contact and mechanical attachment:

> "a proceeding winding of the hemmed edge contacts a succeeding winding of the hemmed edge"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0004]

> "the collector is in electrical communication and mechanically attached to the hemmed edge"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0004]

The description matches the claim: the hems "may be compressed such that each hem is in contact with the hem adjacent to it" [0019], and the hems are there "for making an electrical connection to the cell terminals" [0019].

Is the cell tabbed or tabless? Function is the tab's replacement. Category is tabless. Shape is a continuous folded rim, winding contacting winding, not a local tongue welded at one spot.

The drawings show the geometry as teardrop loops at successive winding positions, with collectors, insulator, and lid closing the can around the wound assembly [0035] [0036] [0037]. The document says the hemmed electrode can improve internal electrical resistance "by minimizing geometric bottlenecks (e.g., electron travel distance) from cell terminals and electrochemically-active material sites throughout the electrode assembly" [0025]. That is a can-claim. No resistance number appears in the document.

## The Equipment List Reads Like a Flag-Line Invoice

Production tabless lines cut the bare foil edge into flags. The trade press describes the practice directly: "the tab extends along the entire battery foil, with a slit cut between each flag," and a tabless design increases the total number of cuts. Equipment vendors sell 46-series laser notching and winding machines as a product category for those lines. Samsung SDI has announced 46-series cylindrical cells on a tabless design. The category is no longer Tesla-only.

Against that baseline, the filing's sharpest paragraph is not a claim chart. It is an equipment list:

> "the machine for making a hemmed electrode may not necessarily require the use of a tabbing device, slitting device, notching device, flag interleaving device, or debris handling device to eliminate foil near the core and/or can edges, and/or exhaust systems for all of these devices"
>
> US 2026/0196678 A1, [0025]

The filing never names the 4680, Tesla's own 2019 tabless patent, or any production line. Its Background attacks only tabs [0002]. Reading the device list as a critique of the industry's cut-edge practice is still the essay's mapping. The inference stands on the document's own words.

The names map onto production jobs. A tabbing device belongs to the old attach-a-tab line. A flag interleaving device is flag-line specific; it has no job on a tab-only line. Slitting and notching also serve other jobs on other lines. The patent's purpose clause says the five devices are not necessarily required to eliminate foil near the core and can edges. Debris handling rides with the cutting. A "flag interleaving device" cannot refer to a tab welder.

The document also says "the hems do not require slits to form the hemmed electrode" [0042], which can "increase the structural integrity of the hemmed electrode and mitigate bending and tearing damage under tensile loads" [0042]. And "the wound hemmed electrode assembly does not include any additional geometries associated with the addition of foil material (e.g. “tabs”) or the subtraction of foil material (e.g. “flags”)" [0038]. Neither add a tab nor subtract a flag. Only fold. The hemmed electrode "can reduce overall costs and solve many manufacturing challenges" [0025], again a can, not a measured saving.

*FIG. 5: method steps 510 to 560. Provide, hem, separator, wind, connect collector, insert into housing. No slitting, notching, or flag step in the box flow.*

Tesla's first tabless patent, filed in 2019 and granted in 2023 as US 11,749,842 B2, described a continuous exposed conductive edge formed by selective coating, not by cutting the foil. Production practice on tabless cylindrical cells still cuts flags. This 2025 filing prices the machine bill of that cut-edge line. Whether Tesla swaps its production process is outside the document. The filing reads as a critique of cutting. It does not say the company has abandoned it.

## The Claims Seek a Fold. The Description Offers a Menu of Shapes

Three independent claims seek the architecture: one for the wound assembly, one for the electrode, one for the method. Claim 1 seeks the wound hemmed assembly with winding-to-winding hem contact and a collector electrically and mechanically attached to the hemmed edge [0004]. Claim 2 seeks an electrode whose foil has a core edge, a can edge, and a hemmed edge between them, with that hemmed edge extending beyond the film's top edge [0005]. Claim 13 seeks a method of providing the film-on-foil electrode and hemming an edge of the foil [0011]. All three are sought scope on a pending application: protection asked for, not yet granted. None pins a numerical dimension.

The description is freer. The document says "the hem may have a teardrop shape in the hemmed electrode prior to winding" [0021]. It "may be connected to the collectors by forced contact, solder joint, brazing, ultrasonic welding, laser welding, adhering, and combinations thereof" [0023]. Film-to-hem separation length, and loop height and width, appear as design menus, not claim pins [0038]. Flattening the hemmed edges "can create a substantially uniform (e.g. flat and void-free) interface for current collector joining" [0047], still an option, not a requirement of the independent claims.

*FIG. 3: two-loop hem variant, both loops bent toward the can side (330-1, 330-2). Shape is description menu, not claim lock.*

*FIG. 4: same two-loop idea, second loop bent back toward the core side (430-1, 430-2). Bend direction alone is the delta.*

What is claimed is that geometry. What is described is a kit of shapes and joining methods around it. Confusing the two would turn options into promises.

## Watch Grant, Continuations, and the Line Itself

Tesla made tabless famous as a manufacturing-simplification story. This next filing keeps the tabless category and replaces cutting with folding. Its own equipment list reads as the bill of machines a flag-cutting line would no longer need. The geometry is sought in three independent claims. The upsides are all can and may.

If these claims are granted in something close to the sought form, that would lock the claimed scope, no more. Two things would carry the mapping: continuation filings that keep the hem-without-slits core, and production-line changes that drop the cutting stack the list names. Companies announce tabless when it is convenient. They file when the edge is already being redesigned on paper.

나열한다. 슬리팅·노칭·flag interleaving·부스러기 처리 장치와 그 배기 설비가 그것이고,
헴 방식은 이런 장비가 필요 없을 수 있다고 대비한다 `[0025]`. 또 헴은 슬릿이 필요 없어서,
슬릿을 쓰는 방식이 감수하는 인장 하중에서의 굽힘·찢김 손상 문제를 피한다고 서술한다
`[0042]`. 다만 본문에 인용 선행문헌 목록은 없고, 이 대비는 명세서 자체 서술이다.

**근거 (verbatim):**
- `[0002]`: "electrode tabs electrically connect the electrodes to exterior terminals of the housing"
  (종래에는 전극 탭이 전극과 하우징 외부 단자를 전기적으로 잇는다는 뜻)
- `[0025]`: "the machine for making a hemmed electrode may not necessarily require the use of a tabbing device, slitting device, notching device, flag interleaving device, or debris handling device"
  (헴 전극 제조기는 태빙·슬리팅·노칭·플래그 삽입·부스러기 처리 장치가 필요 없을 수 있다는 뜻)
- `[0042]`: "the hems do not require slits to form the hemmed electrode"
  (헴 전극을 만드는 데에는 슬릿이 필요 없다는 뜻)

## ③ 이 특허의 독창적 해법 (핵심 청구항 중심)

독립항은 셋이다. 장치(1항), 전극(2항), 방법(13항). 출원 건이므로 locked 는 없고, 아래
필수 요소는 전부 sought-locked(청구 중, 확정 아님)다.

- **1항(장치)**: 헴 전극과 분리막을 감은 조립체가 하우징 안에 있고, 헴 가장자리의 앞
  감김(proceeding winding)과 뒤 감김(succeeding winding)이 서로 접촉하며 `[0004]`,
  컬렉터(collector)가 그 헴 가장자리에 전기적으로 통하고 기계적으로 붙어 있을 것을
  요구한다 `[0004]`.
- **2항(전극)**: 필름이 포일 위에 있고, 포일에 core edge · can edge · 그 사이의 hemmed
  edge 가 있으며, 헴 가장자리가 전극 필름 상단보다 위로 나와 있을 것을 요구한다 `[0005]`.
- **13항(방법)**: 필름 입힌 포일 전극을 준비하고 포일 가장자리를 접어(hemming) 헴 전극을
  만들 것만 요구한다 `[0011]`.

청구가 요구하는 것과 명세서 선호를 섞지 말 것. 물방울(teardrop) 모양 `[0021]`, 1~2중
루프와 굽힘 방향, 헴 롤러 `[0012]`, 평탄화 `[0047]`, 절연 테이프 `[0024]`, 리드·캔의
컬렉터 겸용 `[0022]` 은 종속항이거나 명세서가 열어 둔 선택지(open)다. "about X" 형태의
점 한정(pinned)은 독립항에 없다. `[0038]`의 치수 목록(A·B·C)은 명세서의 메뉴일 뿐이다.

**근거 (verbatim):**
- `[0004]`: "a proceeding winding of the hemmed edge contacts a succeeding winding of the hemmed edge"
  (감긴 상태에서 헴 가장자리의 앞 감김과 뒤 감김이 서로 접촉한다는 뜻)
- `[0004]`: "the collector is in electrical communication and mechanically attached to the hemmed edge"
  (컬렉터가 헴 가장자리에 전기적으로 통하고 기계적으로 부착된다는 뜻)
- `[0005]`: "the hemmed edge extends beyond a top edge of the electrode film"
  (헴 가장자리가 전극 필름 상단보다 위로 나와 있다는 뜻)

## ④ 기대 효과

전부 명세서 주장("can/may")이며 측정 수치는 없다. 제조 측면에서는 탭·슬릿 계열 장비의
제거로 전체 비용을 줄이고 여러 제조 난제(장비 복잡도·설치 면적·에너지·스크랩·인력,
품질·웹 핸들링·기하 정밀도·전해액 주입 시간)를 푼다고 주장한다 `[0025]`. 성능 측면에서는
셀 단자에서 활물질까지의 전자 이동 거리 병목을 최소화해 내부 전기 저항을 개선할 수 있다고
하고 `[0025]`, 안전 측면에서는 열폭주 가스 배출 경로와 내부 여유 공간을 든다 `[0025]`.
부수 효과로, 슬릿 없는 헴의 구조 강성 `[0042]`, 필름과 헴 사이 설계 간격의 열 노출 감소
`[0038]`, 평탄화 시 공극 없는 접합면 `[0047]` 이 서술된다.

**근거 (verbatim):**
- `[0025]`: "the hemmed electrode can reduce overall costs and solve many manufacturing challenges"
  (헴 전극이 전체 비용을 줄이고 여러 제조 난제를 풀 수 있다는 뜻)
- `[0025]`: "the hemmed electrode can improve battery performance (e.g., internal electrical resistance) by minimizing geometric bottlenecks (e.g., electron travel distance) from cell terminals and electrochemically-active material sites throughout the electrode assembly"
  (전자 이동 거리 병목을 줄여 내부 저항 성능을 개선할 수 있다는 뜻)
- `[0025]`: "the hemmed electrode can improve battery safety by superior runaway gas egress channels and free internal overhead volumes"
  (가스 배출 경로와 내부 여유 공간으로 안전성을 개선할 수 있다는 뜻)

## ⑤ 회사 프로모션 글/기술과의 연결

Tesla 는 2020-09-22 Battery Day 에서 4680 셀의 "tabless" 전극을 제조 단순화·저항
감소 서사로 공개했다 (fact-check-log: `battery-day-2020-tabless`, tier-3). 첫
tabless 특허(US20200144676A1, 2019-11-04 출원)는 2023-09-05 에 US11749842B2 로
등록됐고, 그 방식은 포일을 자르지 않는 연속 노출 가장자리다 (fact-check-log:
`tesla-first-tabless-patent-granted`, tier-1). 반면 현재 양산형 tabless 공정은 맨
포일 가장자리를 레이저로 잘라 flag 를 만드는 방식이 업계 표준이고 (46시리즈용
"레이저 노칭+와인딩 통합기"가 장비 상품군으로 존재; Samsung SDI 도 46시리즈
tabless 양산을 공식 발표 — fact-check-log: `tabless-flag-slit-practice`,
`notching-machine-product-category`, `samsung-sdi-46-tabless`), 이번 출원의
`[0025]` 장비 목록(태빙·슬리팅·노칭·flag interleaving·부스러기 처리)은 바로 그
공정의 기계 목록과 겹친다. 프로모 연결 고리: "2020년의 tabless 서사(자르기) →
2026년의 hem 출원(접기)" — Battery Day 서사와 이번 출원의 장비 목록 사이의 긴장이
에세이의 훅이자 프로모의 리드다. 개선 폭 수치(저항 몇 % 등)는 문서에 없으므로
프로모에서도 사용 금지.
