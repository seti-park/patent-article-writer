# Cold Reader — Round 2 (casual scroller)

Read once, top to bottom, the way I actually read a feed article. Clicked because it's Intel + memory. Here's the honest version.

## 1. Where I stopped / started skimming

I did NOT bail — I got to the end. But there's a clear stretch where I stopped *reading* and started *skimming for the good lines*.

**First real slide:** the paragraph in the "Claim 1 Turns on a Single Word" section that starts —

> "In an ordinary memory chip, that transistor is etched into the crystalline silicon at the base of the die, the layer engineers call the front-end. The back-end is everything stacked above it..."

That's where the vocabulary starts stacking up (front-end / back-end / thin-film transistor / deposited as a thin coating). I was still nodding along but I could feel it getting heavier.

**Full skim kicks in here** — the tower section, this sentence exactly:

> "Data drops down the stack through what the patent calls TSV gutters: columns of through-silicon vias punched straight through each die."

From there through the base-die paragraph (TSV gutters → sub-channels → UCIe → spare arrays), I was reading maybe half the words, hunting for something to grab onto. That whole "how it's built inside" middle is the low point. It's plumbing.

**And I skipped every figure caption with the little part numbers.** Anything like "(161A to 161H)... (162 to 165)" or "(151, 153)... (152, 154)" — my eyes just slid off the parentheses every single time. I never once read those.

**Where it pulled me back:** the "Could Loosen the DRAM-Fab Bottleneck" section. The moment it got back to *three companies make this, a foundry could make a fourth*, I was fully back in. The ending held me.

One small drag near the end: the "ZAM is not the same as this filing" caveat gets made like three or four times (claim scope, then again in the bear case, then again in the conclusion). By the third time I got it, and the repeat made the last section feel a touch slower than it needed to.

## 2. What I felt

- **Hooked** at the top — "A Memory Architecture From the Company That Left It" is a great line, and "You change what kind of factory can build it" made me want to keep going.
- **Impressed / trusting** when it flat-out said "the leap is mine, not the document's." That bought a lot of goodwill — didn't feel like it was overselling.
- **Bored / glazed** through the tower-internals middle. Not confused exactly, just tuned out. Too much part-by-part plumbing back to back.
- **Not suspicious** — if anything the opposite. Felt honest.
- **Re-hooked** by "a fourth door." That's the image that stuck.

## 3. The one thing I'd repeat to a friend

"Intel patented a way to build the AI-memory chips that only three companies can make right now — in the chip's wiring instead of the silicon — so a regular chip foundry could maybe crack that bottleneck, if the yield actually works out."
