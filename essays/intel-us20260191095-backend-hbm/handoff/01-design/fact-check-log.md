## Notes

- **Load-bearing vs color.** `hbm-supply-concentration`, `dram-three-player`, `intel-exited-memory`, `zam-hb3dm-specs`, `zam-powerchip-fab`, `incumbent-3d-dram`, and `imec-2t0c-igzo` are load-bearing (Axis 4 baseline, the §1 hook, and the §5 steelman / measured-verdict guards). `zam-hb3dm-vlsi2026` and `zam-timeline` set the falsifiable proof-point calendar the measured verdict points to. `intel-foundry-hbm-basedie` is supporting color only.
- **Connect, do not conflate (guard).** ZAM / HB3DM shares this filing's DIRECTION (tall custom 3D DRAM to beat HBM4; base die + high-speed I/O) but differs in the headline interconnect: this patent = vertical TSV gutters + UCIe; public ZAM = diagonal "Z-angle" + hybrid bonding. Correct posture: same-family cousin / plausibly related IP, NOT confirmed identical. Never write "this patent is ZAM."
- **`intel-foundry-hbm-basedie` caveat.** Both direct searches for the Foundry Direct Connect base-die roadmap returned transient "web search unavailable" this session; the fact is carried from the orchestrator's seed at trade-press level and was not independently re-verified. Use as one-clause color at most, or drop; re-verify before any load-bearing use. The source URL is a best-effort placeholder for the Tom's Hardware Foundry Direct Connect 2025 coverage — Phase 2/3 should confirm the exact article URL if the fact is used.
- **Numbers that must stay the essay's, never the patent's.** All HBM/DRAM shares, ZAM specs (bandwidth, layers, TSV counts, die area, capacity, power), the $559M write-off, and the incumbent-roadmap dates are external. The patent gives NO cost, yield, retention, $-per-bit, or bandwidth-vs-HBM4 figure. Where the essay compares "0.5-5 GB per die" (patent) to ZAM's "~10 GB module" (external), the two must be clearly labeled as patent-side vs news-side.
